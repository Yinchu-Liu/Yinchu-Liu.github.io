About Me

Hello! I'm Yinchu Liu, a third-year PhD student supervised by Prof. Sheldon Andrews and Prof. Kenny Erleben. My expertise spans rigid body simulation, collision detection, graph partitioning, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.

I'm passionate about developing advanced algorithms for computer graphics and physics-based simulations. My research focuses on using machine learning techniques to enhance numerical solvers, as well as to improve both discrete (DCD) and continuous collision detection (CCD), aiming to increase the efficiency and accuracy of complex simulations.

Outside of research and coding, I enjoy exploring new technologies and contributing to open-source projects in computer graphics and simulation.

I am currently open for internship opportunities and research collaborations!
